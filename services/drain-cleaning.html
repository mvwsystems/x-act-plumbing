Common Questions

Drain Cleaning FAQ

What's the difference between hydro-jetting and snaking?

A drain snake breaks up clogs mechanically but leaves residue on pipe walls. Hydro-jetting uses high-pressure water to completely clear the pipe interior — grease, scale, roots, and debris — restoring flow to near-original capacity.

When should I call a plumber for a clogged drain?

If a plunger hasn't resolved the issue, or multiple drains are slow at the same time, you likely have a blockage deeper in the line. Recurring clogs in the same drain are also a sign of a deeper problem worth diagnosing.

Do you do camera inspections before drain cleaning?

Yes. For persistent or recurring clogs, we use camera inspection to diagnose exactly what's in the line before recommending a solution. This prevents unnecessary work and gets the fix right the first time.
